Shortening

The shortening process is based mostly on a kind of linguistic laziness called clipping that causes us to lop off great chunks of words. For example, we end up with fridge from refrigerator and flu from influenza. Often we clip everything after the first syllable: dis
A related process is the creation of acronyms, which form a pronounceable word using the first letters of each word in a phrase. For example, UNICEF from United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund, and NATO from North Atlantic Treaty Organization.
